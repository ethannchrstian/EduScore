# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BINUS university students, using the app on their phone between and around classes. They juggle several courses (matakuliah), each with tasks (tugas) and study sessions they want to log without friction. Their context is short, frequent visits: check what's due, mark something done, log study time, get out. They are not power administrators and they are not browsing for fun, they have a specific thing to record or check each time they open it.

## Product Purpose

EduScore is a mobile-first academic tracker that lets a student see their coursework, deadlines, and study time in one place and keep it current with minimal effort. It exists because university portals and LMS tools are built for institutions, not for a student's day-to-day "what do I do next" question. Success looks like a student trusting it enough to log tasks and study sessions habitually, and being able to answer "what's due and how am I doing" in a single glance.

## Brand Personality

Sharp and efficient. A power tool for students: fast, precise, tap-efficient, log-and-go. The voice is direct and concrete, never chatty or motivational-poster. Confident without being corporate. Three words: **fast, clear, dependable.** Emotionally it should reduce the friction (and low-grade anxiety) of staying on top of coursework, not amplify it with noise or guilt.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI SaaS template.** No indigo-gradient hero, no big-number hero-metric blocks, no endless grid of identical icon+heading+text cards, no decorative glassmorphism. If a screen could be any AI-generated dashboard, it has failed.
- **Cluttered legacy LMS (e.g. the BINUS portal itself).** No everything-on-one-screen overload, no dense nested menus, no institutional-form feel. The product earns its place by being the calm, focused alternative to the portal.

## Design Principles

- **Log-and-go.** Optimize every flow for the shortest path from intent to recorded. Minimize taps to add a task, mark it done, or log a study session.
- **Earn every element.** Reject the SaaS scaffold by default. Show only what helps a student decide their next action; cut anything decorative or template-shaped.
- **Glanceable status.** "What's due" and "how am I doing" should be answerable in under a second, without scrolling or interpretation.
- **Focused, not crowded.** Be the antidote to the LMS. Enough density to be useful in one screen, never the overwhelming all-at-once dump.
- **Motion with a job.** The existing completion/check animations confirm and reward real actions. Motion stays purposeful; it never decorates an idle screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort, not a formal conformance target. Aim for readable body contrast (don't ship light-gray text on tinted near-white), visible focus states for keyboard/switch users, and reduced-motion alternatives for the animation-heavy interactions (task-complete burst, big-check overlay, staggered reveals), since motion is core to the feel. No strict WCAG audit required for now, but don't regress these basics.
